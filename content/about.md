---
title: "About the Workshop"
---

As foundation models continue to advance in capability, intelligent systems are increasingly moving beyond offline analysis and purely cloud-based services toward sustained interaction, online decision-making, and physical execution in the real world. In this transition, a cloud-only paradigm is becoming less able to meet practical requirements such as low latency, privacy protection, safety and reliability, and continued operation under limited connectivity.

This workshop treats on-device intelligence not as a mere extension of model compression or deployment, but as a tightly coupled research problem spanning theory, algorithms, systems, and evaluation — where capability must be considered jointly with efficiency, adaptation, execution, and reliability. For VLAs, robotics, and autonomous platforms, latency directly affects control stability and operational safety.

Recent advances in efficient foundation models, multimodal systems, embodied intelligence, and edge hardware are progressing rapidly but largely in parallel, with limited shared abstractions and evaluation practices. Our goal is to create a shared venue for discussion and community building *before* these communities harden into separate problem formulations — helping clarify common constraints, identify key research questions, and shape a coherent research agenda around private, low-latency, reliable, and safe intelligence on real devices.

Join us for keynote talks, a panel discussion, contributed oral presentations, and two poster sessions.
